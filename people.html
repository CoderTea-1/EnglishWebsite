People Change

By: Dawn Lambert




Reflection

The poem, “People Change”, is a deep reflection by the author about how people evolve over time, sometimes how you expect and others in ways you didn’t expect. It is a great viewpoint into the author’s thought process. It delves into great detail about the emotional turmoil of seeing people change, and knowing there is nothing you can do to stop it. It touches on the profound fear of the unknown. The poem also has an interesting part of the author having this moment of reckoning where they question whether the person changed, or if they are just revealing their true self. We often build trust on what we know, thus making change something that makes us lose trust. This leads to a sense of betrayal and discomfort. Overall, this poem paints a picture that I deeply empathize with. High school is where much of this happens. As people grow older, they mature and change in different ways. In my personal experience, I have seen this maturity lead to people creating a shell around themselves, becoming hostile to others. On the other hand, I have seen people I hate become better people, which has pushed me to rethink whether I should continue to have a grudge or not. For me, the biggest problem is still this sense of remembering the past, which shadows our judgment. I also have a deep appreciation for the author digging at the idea of a “mask”. For example, I have seen someone I know in this English class change in ways that makes me want to push them away. In the past, I saw them as this person who is always picked on, but has a deep drive for knowledge. In this class, this image has burned down, where it seems like they are spiraling for the worse. To this day, I don’t know if I am seeing a more real version of them or if this is a part of maturing.
